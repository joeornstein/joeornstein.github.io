Introduction to Political Methodology, Week 1

  • Aug 15, 2020
  • Categories: R , Statistics , Political Science , Education
  • One minute read

My first-semester graduate political methodology course is a practical introduction to working with data. We’ll cover the most useful mathematical and computational tools that one needs to function as an academic political scientist, including:

  • Data Wrangling
  • Data Visualization
  • Reproducible Research
  • Optimization
  • Matrix Algebra
  • Probability and Inference

In Week 1, we discuss the fundamentals of R, functions, vectors, matrices, and summary statistics. The slides are here.
