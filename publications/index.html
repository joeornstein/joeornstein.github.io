Articles

  1. Stacked Regression and Poststratification Political Analysis (2020), 28:293-301
    [PDF] [Appendix] [Replication Materials] [R Package]
    TLDR: Combining ensemble models (stacking) with postratification improves public opinion estimates.

  2. Moving From Metrics to Mechanisms to Evaluate Tobacco Retailer Policies: Importance of Retail Policy in Tobacco Control (with Doug Luke, Todd Combs, Lisa Henriksen, and Maggie Mahoney). American Journal of Public Health (2020), 110(4):431-432.
    [PDF] [Dashboard]
    TLDR: Lowering the density of tobacco retail doesn’t significantly decrease the average city resident’s proximity to tobacco retail, unless you reduce density by a lot.

  3. The Burglary Boost: A Note On Detecting Contagion Using the Knox Test (with Ross A. Hammond). Journal of Quantitative Criminology (2017), 33(1): 65-75
    [PDF] [NetLogo Code] [R Code]
    TLDR: A standard statistical technique for detecting “contagion” in crime data does not work as advertised.

  4. Frequency of monotonicity failure under Instant Runoff Voting: estimates based on a spatial model of elections (with Robert Norman). Public Choice (2014), 161:1-9 [PDF] [NetLogo Code] [Post-Publication Comments]
    TLDR: Instant Runoff Voting (aka the Alternative Vote, aka Ranked Choice Voting) is suffers from monotonicity paradoxes when IRV eliminates the Condorcet winner in a competitive threeway election. This occurs about 25% of the time in simulations. Use a Condorcet-consistent ranked choice system instead and those paradoxes are almost entirely eliminated.

  5. A Model of Social Influence on Body Mass Index (with Ross A. Hammond). Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences (2014), 1331:34-42

  6. A Model of Food Reward Learning with Dynamic Reward Exposure (with Ross A. Hammond, et al.) Frontiers in Computational Neuroscience (2012), 6:82
    TLDR: A computational temporal difference learning (TDL) model suggests that early exposure to healthy foods can have long-run effects on preferences.
